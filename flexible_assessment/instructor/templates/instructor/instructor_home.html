Welcome to Flexible Assessment, a Canvas integration that allows students to choose how their final grades will be weighted within ranges defined by the instructor.

This system was created for a Teaching & Learning Enhancement Fund (TLEF) project to streamline the processes involved in Flexible Assessment, including: Collecting desired percentages from students, recalculating their final grades according to those percentages, and exporting the grades into the Canvas Grades page. It is based on a Flexible Assessment approach devised by Dr. Candice Rideout, a Professor of Teaching in the Faculty of Land & Food Systems that has been shown to increase student satisfaction and self-regulation [1].

For more information, see our About page.

[1] Candice A. Rideout (2018) Students' choices and achievement in large undergraduate classes using a novel flexible assessment approach, Assessment & Evaluation in Higher Education, 43:1, 68-78, DOI: 10.1080/02602938.2017.1294144

Instructions

  1. Setup

    1. Go to the Assessments page and enter assessments as they appear on your syllabus.
    2. For each assessment you will have to add the default, minimum and maximum weights you will accept.
    3. Add open and close dates for when students can enter their desired percentages.
    4. Clicking Save completes the setup. You can view the assessments and availability dates under Assessments.

  2. Review

    1. Your students enter their desired percentages and comments that you can review on the Student Choices page as the term progresses.
    2. Students who do not enter desired percentages will use the default values.

  3. Final Grades

    1. Go to Assignments on Canvas and create your Assignment Groups (e.g. Assignments, Quizzes, Midterm, etc.) and within Canvas add the graded assessments to their respective groups if you haven't done so already.
    2. On Canvas go to Grades > Settings > Advanced and check Allow final grade override. This allows the app to upload the recalculated grades to Canvas.
    3. After all assessments have been graded, go to the Final Grades page and match your Assignment Groups on Canvas to your assessments in the app. Our app receives the final grades for each Canvas Assignment Group. Note: Individual assignments in the same Canvas Assignment Group are weighted by their maximum point value, so an assignment worth 20 points is weighted more than an assignment worth 10 points. Please verify that Canvas has calculated grades for each Assignment Group properly.
    4. You can view a summary of final grades to verify that everything is correct before submitting grades to Canvas or exporting as a CSV.

Please contact it@landfood.ubc.ca for more information.
